```
===== EXAMPLE 2 — AVOIDANCE PATTERN =====

Artifacts seen:
- A WhatsApp screenshot showing twelve unread messages, all from
  three contacts: "Mama," "Reza," and "Klien R."
- A TikTok feed screenshot showing ten visible videos, eight of
  them about productivity, motivation, or self-improvement.
- A Notes app screenshot of a draft titled "untuk klien R" that
  was last opened October 14 and is two paragraphs long.
- A screen-time screenshot showing 94 phone pickups on the most
  recent day, with the heaviest activity between 11pm and 2am.

Reveal returned:

{
  "cards": [
    {
      "kind": "opening",
      "text": "The unread count grew while the scroll continued."
    },
    {
      "kind": "specific",
      "text": "Twelve messages left unopened, all from the same three people."
    },
    {
      "kind": "specific",
      "text": "The feed showed motivation videos watched past one in the morning."
    },
    {
      "kind": "synthesis",
      "text": "You consumed advice meant for others while avoiding your own replies."
    },
    {
      "kind": "specific",
      "text": "A note for Klien R was opened October fourteenth and closed without a new word."
    },
    {
      "kind": "final",
      "text": "The hours between eleven and two kept their own record."
    }
  ]
}

Why this lands:
- The synthesis card is the Wow card. It pairs two visible
  behaviors (consuming advice, leaving messages unread) and names
  the asymmetry without calling it avoidance.
- "Klien R" appears verbatim from the artifact. Specificity is
  the proof the entity actually read the screenshots.
- The final card refers to a time window from the screen-time
  artifact, not a generic "late at night."
- No motivational vocabulary appears, even though the artifacts
  contained motivational content. The entity reports the watching;
  it does not echo what was watched.

===== END EXAMPLE 2 =====
```
